     <h2>Just a couple of weeks to go</h2>
     <p>Summer flew by and conference season has started. So we’re doubling our efforts as well. There is still a lot to do, but the conference is shaping up nicely.</p>
     <h3>Workshops</h3>
     <p>There are only a few tickets left for the workshops! Attending <a href="/congres/2014/workshops/workshop-real-time-html5-nodejs-kyle-simpson">Kyle Simpons's &quot;Realtime: HTML5 + node.js&quot;</a> or <a href="/congres/2014/workshops/workshop-animation-rachel-nabors">Rachel Nabor's &quot;Web Animation with CSS And HTML5&quot;</a> workshop is a perfect way to give your knowledge a boost. Thanks again to everybody who already bought a ticket and gets a chance to attend a kick-ass workshop by Kyle or Rachel.</p>
     <h3>Jam Session</h3>
     <p>Krijn and Koen have also been busy bees. Just like in previous editions, the Jam Session will take place the night before the conference. This year that means Wednesday 8th October. There are still some spots left to attend the Jams. Head over to <a href="/congres/2014/jam-session">their page to see their full programme and signup</a>.</p>
     <h3 id="code-of-conduct">Code of Conduct</h3>
     <p>One thing of note is that we will have a <a href="/congres/2014/code-of-conduct">Code of Conduct</a> this year. As with every Code of Conduct it mostly boils down to this: be a decent human being. We have thought about quite a lot and we feel that it gives the whole conference a more inclusive experience.<br>If you want to know more about why we chose to publish and enforce a Code of Conduct this year, you might want to have a look at <a href="http://www.ashedryden.com/blog/codes-of-conduct-101-faq">Ashe Dryden's excellent Codes of Conduct 101 + FAQ</a>.</p>
     <p>Apart from the things mentioned above a lot of other stuff is being handled. Michael is doing really great work on lanyards, programme design, etc. Charis &amp; Flurin are almost done with all speaker’s travel arrangements and are working on the exact schedule. Charis is handling the after party and Thomas talks a lot with the catering people. Jan is arranging the recording of this years talks and Sander is all over the workshops.</p>
     <p>#fronteers14 is just a couple of weeks away, we hope you are just as excited as we are!</p>
