     <h2>Fronteers 2010 ticket sale, speakers and workshops (27-04-2010)</h2>
     <p>Today we're starting to sell tickets for the Fronteers 2010 conference which is to be held on October 7 and 8 (Fronteers members, who get a discount, will receive an e-mail about this), announcing two new speakers, and revealing some of our plans regarding workshops.</p>
     <h3>Speakers</h3>
     <p>After Jeremy Keith and Jeff Croft we're pleased to announce that <strong><a href="http://www.themaninblue.com/">Cameron Adams</a></strong> and <strong><a href="http://www.nczonline.net/">Nicholas Zakas</a></strong> have agreed to speak at our conference. Cameron is the guy you should go to about <a href="http://www.themaninblue.com/experiment/">interesting experiments inside the browser</a> or <a href="http://www.themaninblue.com/writing/perspective/2010/03/22/">comparing studies</a> between SVG, <code>&lt;canvas&gt;</code> and Flash. Nicholas is a big name in the area of <a href="http://www.nczonline.net/writing/">JavaScript performance</a> and will tell us more about this and keeping your user interface snappy.</p>
     <p>In total you can expect at least 14 <em>unsponsored</em> sessions.</p>
     <h3>Workshops</h3>
     <p>We haven't put much focus on the area of design during our previous conferences, but for this year we've decided to host a number of design-related workshops prior to the conference. Andy Clarke will give his <a href="/congres/2010/workshops/advanced-css-andy-clarke">Advanced CSS styling</a> workshop in the <a href="/congres/2010/workshops#venue">Kremlin room in Felix Meritis</a> on Wednesday the 6th of October. Thirty seats are available for this workshop, so you can expect a more personal approach than the conference itself. The focus of this intensive workshop lies on one specific subject and you'll leave the day with a pile of new knowledge.</p>
     <p>At this moment we're in the middle of contacting other workshoppers, so perhaps the Tuesday and Monday will be filled with workshops as well.</p>
     <h3>Ticket sale</h3>
     <p>Today we're also starting with the early-bird ticket sale. Just like last year we're using <a href="http://paydro.net/">Paydro</a> for this. You can pay with creditcard, iDEAL or on invoice. The ticket prices are a bit higher than last year, since we don't have any sponsortalks this year and <a href="/congres/2010/venue">Tuschinski</a> is a bit pricier (but it's an <em>awesome</em> venue).</p>

     <h3>Sponsorships</h3>
     <p>We're still looking for <a href="/congres/2010/sponsorships">some sponsors</a> for this year. If you're interested or think that your boss should attend, let us know and you'll be a VIP during the speakers dinner on Wednesday.</p>
     <h3>Follow us on Twitter</h3>
     <p>We'll use our <a href="https://twitter.com/FronteersConf">@FronteersConf</a> twitter account to keep up a running stream of minor updates, so if you're not following us yet, you know what to do.</p>
     <p>Of course you can also subscribe to our <a href="http://feeds.feedburner.com/FronteersCongres">RSS feed</a>, or <a href="/congres#per-mail">leave your e-mail address</a> to receive updates in your inbox.</p>
     <h3>That's it</h3>
     <p>For now this is it; it's up to you now. Please help us promote our conference on your blog, Twitter or simply around the watercooler at work and let's together make sure <strong>Fronteers 2010</strong> will once again be an impressive, memorable, unique kick-ass conference!</p>
     <p>If you have any questions or remarks, like everything with Fronteers, please don't hesitate to get yourself heard!</p>
