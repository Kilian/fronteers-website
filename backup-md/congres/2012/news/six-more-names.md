     <h2>Six more names (05-09-2012)</h2>
     <p><strong>We are proud to announce that we have added a few more names to our line-up: Mark Boulton, Peter Nederlof and Alex Graul have all agreed to give us a presentation. In addition, there will be an accessibility-panel with Bram Duvigneau, Bor Verkroost and Antoine Hegeman.</strong></p>
     <p>On the <a href="/congres/2012/speakers">list of speakers</a> which have been announced so far, you could have seen them already: Addy Osmani, Anne van Kesteren, David DeSandro, Marcin Wichary, Mathias Bynens, Lea Verou, Phil Hawksworth and Rebecca Murphey.</p>
     <p><strong><a href="http://www.markboulton.co.uk/">Mark Boulton</a></strong> runs a little design studio and has a lot of experience with very demanding websites. He is interested in the intersection between front-end and design. How can you easily apply design for developers? Wednesday before the conference, Mark also gives a <a href="/congres/2012/workshops/visual-design-101-for-web-developers-mark-boulton">workshop</a>.</p>
     <p><strong><a href="http://peterned.home.xs4all.nl/">Peter Nederlof</a></strong> is a front-end developer pur sang and always busy with the latest techniques, whether it is a 3D engine in canvas or a CSS matrix generator. Finally we convinced him to enter the Tuschinski stage.</p>
     <p><strong><a href="http://www.sho.ch/">Alex Graul</a></strong> has specialized in data visualisation. How do you communicate data while keeping it interesting? Alex is also co-developer of <a href="http://misoproject.com/">Miso</a>, &quot;an open source toolkit designed to expedite the creation of high-quality interactive storytelling and data visualisation content&quot;.</p>
     <p><strong><a href="https://twitter.com/bramduvigneau">Bram Duvigneau</a></strong>, <strong><a href="http://www.eborfoundation.com/">Bor Verkroost</a></strong> and <strong>Antoine Hegeman</strong>,all user experts on the (in)accessibility of the web, will be giving practical examples of the pitfalls they encounter on the web during our Accessibility panel. In other words: this year no presentation about how the web <em>should be</em> in theory, but how it is, <em>right now</em>. The panel is moderated by Chris Heilmann.</p>
     <p>Ticket sales for fronteers 2012 have closed, but we've managed to scrounge up a few more tickets. On September 22nd, roughly precisely around 12:00 CET we will sell the last ten tickets. So be quick.</p>
     <p>And whether you come to the conference or not, there are still tickets available for the workshop <em>Visual Design 101 for Web Developers</em> by Mark Boulton. All basics of a good design are covered. <a href="/congres/2012/workshops/visual-design-101-for-web-developers-mark-boulton">More information and ticket sales</a>. This way you can also get access to the conference.</p>
     <p>We still have a month to go and a lot of preparations to make, but we're looking forward to it!</p>
